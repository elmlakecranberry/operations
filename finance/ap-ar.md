# Accounts Payable and Accounts Receivable

This document describes how accounts payable (AP) and accounts receivable (AR) are managed at Elm Lake Cranberry.

## Accounts Payable

Accounts payable covers money owed to vendors, suppliers, and service providers.

### Where AP Is Managed

- **QuickBooks Online** is the system of record for bills and vendor balances
- **Bank portals** are used for payment execution (checks, ACH, wires)
- **Vendor invoices** are received via mail, email, or vendor portals

### AP Workflow

1. Invoice is received from vendor
2. Invoice is reviewed for accuracy (quantities, pricing, terms)
3. Invoice is matched to purchase order or approved expense (if applicable)
4. Invoice is entered into QBO
5. Payment is scheduled based on terms and cash availability
6. Payment is approved per approval thresholds
7. Payment is executed via appropriate method (check, ACH, wire)
8. Payment is recorded in QBO and matched to the bill

### Approval Thresholds

Payment approvals follow management-defined thresholds:

| Payment Amount | Approval Required |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Routine operating expenses | Bookkeeper or designated approver |
| Above routine threshold | Manager approval |
| Large or unusual payments | Owner approval |

Specific dollar thresholds are defined in management documentation.

### Vendor Management

- New vendors require verification before first payment
- Vendor payment information (address, ACH details) is maintained in QBO
- Changes to vendor payment information require verification to prevent fraud

## Accounts Receivable

Accounts receivable covers money owed to ELC by customers.

### Where AR Is Managed

- **QuickBooks Online** is the system of record for invoices and customer balances
- **Bank accounts** receive customer payments
- **Customer communication** occurs via email or phone for payment follow-up

### AR Workflow

1. Service is provided or product is delivered
2. Invoice is created in QBO
3. Invoice is sent to customer
4. Payment is received (check, ACH, wire)
5. Payment is deposited to bank account
6. Payment is recorded in QBO and matched to the invoice
7. Outstanding balances are monitored and followed up as needed

### Collections

For overdue accounts:

- Review aging report regularly to identify overdue balances
- Contact customer for payment status
- Escalate to management for significant or persistent non-payment
- Document collection efforts

Collection actions beyond routine follow-up require management direction.

## Reporting

Standard AP/AR reports include:

| Report | Purpose | Frequency |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Accounts Payable Aging | Shows outstanding bills by age | Weekly or as needed |
| Accounts Receivable Aging | Shows outstanding invoices by age | Weekly or as needed |
| Cash Flow Forecast | Projects cash needs based on AP/AR | As needed |
| Vendor Spending Summary | Analyzes spending by vendor | Monthly or quarterly |

Reports are generated from QBO and shared with management as needed.

## Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | AP Responsibilities | AR Responsibilities |
|------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Bookkeeper | Enter bills, schedule payments, reconcile | Create invoices, record payments, reconcile |
| Manager | Approve payments above threshold | Approve credit terms, direct collection efforts |
| Owner | Approve large payments, vendor relationships | Major customer relationships, write-off decisions |

## AI Boundaries

AI may assist with:

- Reviewing AP/AR aging reports for anomalies
- Analyzing spending patterns from exported data
- Identifying unusual transactions for human review
- Drafting collection correspondence for human review and sending

AI does not:

- Execute payments
- Send invoices or collection notices without human approval
- Approve expenses or credits
- Access banking systems to initiate transactions
- Make decisions about write-offs or payment terms
